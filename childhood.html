A Special Birthday

I came into this world on December 12, 2002 - which coincidentally is Jamhuri Day, Kenya's national holiday celebrating independence. My mother always joked that I was her little independence day gift, arriving right as the nation was celebrating.

Growing Up in Kiserian

I was born and raised in Kiserian, a place that will always feel like home. Our family consisted of my single mother, my older brother, and my younger sister. Being the middle child meant I had to learn to balance looking up to my brother while setting an example for my sister.

Facing Challenges Together

Our childhood wasn't always easy. With just our mother supporting us, we learned the value of hard work and resourcefulness early on. But what we lacked materially, we made up for in love and togetherness.

Some of my strongest memories are of:

  • Sharing one textbook between the three of us for homework
  • Our mother working late shifts but still making time to check our schoolwork
  • My brother protecting us from neighborhood bullies
  • Teaching my sister how to ride a bike with our makeshift version

Unbreakable Bonds

Despite the hardships, our family grew incredibly close. We developed our own traditions:

Sunday Stories

Every Sunday evening, we'd gather to share our week's experiences and dreams

Birthday Celebrations

Even with little money, my mother made sure each birthday felt special with her famous mandazi

Homework Time

The three of us would study together at our small kitchen table every night

These experiences taught me that family isn't about what you have, but who you have.
